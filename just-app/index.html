Two questions everyone asks

"But I have more than 3 things to do."

You do. JUST. is not for all of it.

  • → It's for the 3 things that won't happen unless you actively decide they will.
  • → Your lectures, meetings, obligations have external deadlines. They happen regardless.
  • → JUST. is for the things with no deadline. No one chasing you. Only a slow accumulation of regret if you skip.
  • → A 20-task list doesn't make you 20x more productive. It gives your brain 20 reasons to shut down before you start.

"Why not just use a notebook?"

You can. Four things a notebook cannot do.

  • → Midnight wipe is automatic. A notebook carries everything forward. JUST. deletes at midnight whether you want it to or not.
  • → The limit is enforced. A notebook lets you write a 4th task. Then 15. The constraint only works if it cannot be broken.
  • → Progress and reset are automatic. A notebook can't track your streak or wipe the slate at midnight by itself.
  • → A notebook has no streak to lose. JUST. does. Miss a day and it resets. That feeling of not wanting to break the chain is exactly what keeps you coming back.

The 3 are not your whole day. They're the part of your day that only exists if you choose it.

Why I Built JUST

Productivity culture sold us a lie. More tools. More systems. More integrations. As if the problem was organisation, never discipline. It wasn't. The problem was infinite optionality dressed up as productivity.

JUST exists because constraint is a decision. Three tasks. Midnight wipe. No backlog. Not for aesthetics, but because the brain performs better when the choice is already made. Fewer decisions. More execution.

JUST began as a personal protocol. A way to end the cycle of planning over finishing. The structure worked. The negotiation stopped. This isn't a productivity app. It's a daily commitment mechanism, designed to close the gap between intention and action. Built for people who are done negotiating with themselves.
